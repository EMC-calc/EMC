Basic Proportionality Theorem

Theorem and Proof

Triangle ABC with DE || BC

BPT Theorem Statement

If a line is drawn parallel to one side of a triangle to intersect the other two sides in distinct points, the other two sides are divided in the same ratio.

Given: Δ ABC where DE || BC

To Prove: AD/DB = AE/EC

Proof of BPT

Construction: Join BE and CD. Draw DM ⊥ AC and EN ⊥ AB.

Step 1: Area of triangle ADE:

ar(ADE) = ½ × Base × Height = ½ × AD × EN ... (1)

Area of triangle BDE:

ar(BDE) = ½ × Base × Height = ½ × DB × EN ... (2)

Step 2: Alternative area of triangle ADE:

ar(ADE) = ½ × Base × Height = ½ × AE × DM ... (3)

Area of triangle DEC:

ar(DEC) = ½ × Base × Height = ½ × EC × DM ... (4)

Step 3: Divide (1) and (2):

ar(ADE) / ar(BDE) = (½ × AD × EN) / (½ × DB × EN) = AD / DB ... (A)

Divide (3) and (4):

ar(ADE) / ar(DEC) = (½ × AE × DM) / (½ × EC × DM) = AE / EC ... (B)

Step 4: Triangles BDE and DEC are on the same base DE and between the same parallel lines BC and DE:

∴ ar(BDE) = ar(DEC)

Hence,

ar(ADE) / ar(BDE) = ar(ADE) / ar(DEC)

From (A) and (B):

AD / DB = AE / EC

Hence Proved.
